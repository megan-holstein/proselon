# Chapter Plot Template

Use this template to create an individual chapter plot once the relevant chapter map exists and the Synopsis Cold Read and Plot Stress Test have appended their Chapter Map Rerun sections (a rerun finding can invalidate an act-sized batch — see AGENTS.md, Step 5).

Before filling it, read `Plot/Book N/Story State.md` (if the ledger exists — for Book 2 onward it holds threads carried from the previous book even before any scene of this book completes): Open Threads for payoffs this chapter must carry, and the Characters/Timeline sections for the chapter's Start State and Continuity Inputs when prior chapters are already drafted.

## Interview Goals

Before filling the template, gather enough information to answer:

- What does this chapter need to accomplish in the book?
- What changes between the start and end of the chapter?
- Whose POV carries the chapter?
- What scenes are probably needed?
- What continuity from previous chapters must be honored?
- What must remain unresolved at chapter end?
- What internal conflict is the POV character navigating in this chapter?
- How does the chapter's external pressure connect to the character's psychological tension?

Create chapter plot documents only for chapters covered by the chapter map. Do not create scene plots until the relevant chapter plot exists.

## Completion Criteria

A usable chapter plot document:

- Has a clear chapter function.
- Defines start and end states.
- Lists required beats.
- Identifies likely scenes without drafting them.
- Tracks continuity from prior material.
- Ends with forward momentum.
- Identifies the chapter's internal conflict and how external events pressure it.

**Output saves to:** `Plot/Book N/Chapters/Chapter N/Chapter N Plot.md`
