# Book Plot Template

Use this template to create or revise a book-level, episode-level, novella-level, or installment-level plot.

## Interview Goals

Before filling the template, gather enough information to answer:

- What is this book's function in the larger project?
- What changes between the first page and last page?
- What is the protagonist trying to do externally?
- What emotional, moral, relational, or intellectual arc must happen?
- Which themes from `Plot/Themes and Conflict.md` get pressured most in this book, and which characters carry that pressure?
- Which inner conflicts from `Plot/Themes and Conflict.md` escalate in this book — and what specific book-level events force that escalation?
- What plot mechanics must unfold in order?
- What characters or factions have required roles?
- What must be carried into the next book?
- What decisions are still open?

Do not create separate chapter map or chapter plot files yet. A book plot may include a concise chapter outline, but detailed chapter allocation belongs in the Chapter Map (5.4).

When the book plot saves, also initialize the book's Story State ledger (`Plot/Book N/Story State.md`) from the `5.3 - Story State Template.md`, seeded with the book's starting conditions — and, for Book 2 onward, with the carry-forward state from the previous book's final ledger (see 5.3's Lifecycle). The book plot is not complete without its ledger.

## Candidate Generation

Produce this artifact via candidates, not a single draft (see AGENTS.md › Candidate Generation for Planning Artifacts) — the book plot is the single highest-leverage artifact per book; 100k words of prose inherit whatever it decides. Force the candidates structurally apart in their briefs: different act structures, different inciting incidents, different POV strategies, different crisis placements, one candidate that inverts the expected climax. Author-confirmed decisions and series-plot obligations are constraints every candidate honors; divergence explores what remains open.

## Completion Criteria

A usable book plot document:

- Makes the book's function clear.
- Defines start and end states.
- Tracks all required throughlines.
- Separates plot mechanics from thematic commentary.
- Gives a chapter outline at the level needed for book assessment.
- Records unresolved questions instead of hiding them.
- Connects each active theme to a specific character's internal conflict with an escalation plan.
- Has a matching `Plot/Book N/Story State.md` initialized from the 5.3 template.

**Output saves to:** `Plot/Book N/Book N Plot.md`
