# Scene Plot Template

Use this template to create scene plots once the relevant chapter plot exists.

Scene plots are planning documents. Keep them in `Plot/`, outside exportable manuscript folders.

## Interview Goals

Before filling the template, gather enough information to answer:

- What is the scene's purpose?
- Who is the POV character?
- What does the POV character want in the scene?
- What prevents them from getting it?
- What internal conflict is the POV character caught between in this scene?
- How does the scene's external obstacle connect to the character's psychological tension?
- What changes by the end?
- What information, clue, choice, or emotional beat must land?
- What continuity must the draft preserve?
- What sensory material belongs naturally to this scene?
- Which facts in this scene are already established in prior scenes, and which get their first prose rendering here?

**State source:** Fill the Start State, Continuity Constraints, and the Information Log's Established and Ongoing sections from `Plot/Book N/Story State.md` (the rolling ledger), not by reconstructing state from prior scene plots. The ledger is the authority for current in-story state and fact provenance; prior scene plots are only needed for narrative flow into this scene.

**Act-boundary sweep:** when creating the first scene plot of a new act, first perform the ledger's fact-migration sweep (see the Story State template's Update Discipline) — stable facts move to profiles and `Worldbuilding/` so the Established Facts Index stays scannable.

Do not draft prose during scene plotting unless the author explicitly asks.

## Completion Criteria

A usable scene plot:

- Defines the scene's purpose.
- Gives the POV character a concrete goal.
- Identifies pressure, conflict, or obstacle.
- Names the scene's internal conflict and connects it to the chapter's psychological question.
- Specifies the turn.
- Defines the end state.
- Provides enough sensory and continuity material for drafting.
- Separates established facts from new information so the drafter knows what to render fresh vs. what to treat as backdrop.
- Does not contain manuscript prose unless explicitly requested.
- Has a Production row in `Plot/Book N/Story State.md` with Plot marked.

**Output saves to:** `Plot/Book N/Chapters/Chapter N/S# - Scene Name.md`
