# Chapter Plot Assessment

Evaluate structure within a single chapter — the next unfinished chapter. Run this rubric only when the chapter map is in place.

## Prerequisites

Read these files before assessing:

1. The chapter plot from `Plot/Book N/Chapters/Chapter N/Chapter N Plot.md` (or `Chapter 0 - Prologue/` / `Chapter N+1 - Epilogue/` as appropriate) — the target chapter's planned structure
2. All scene files in the chapter directory (if any exist)
3. The previous chapter's final scene plot (and its manuscript file, if drafted) — for continuity and momentum *(none for the book's first chapter)*
4. `Plot/Specs.md` — word count targets
5. Project character profiles for characters appearing in this chapter *(those that exist)*
6. `Plot/Themes and Conflict.md` — for thematic thread tracking
7. The book plot's Thematic Arc Map section — for the planned internal conflict escalation
8. `Plot/Book N/Story State.md` (if the ledger exists) — the source the writer used for Continuity Inputs; you can't verify continuity claims you never read

Assess against what exists at this point in the workflow. A missing file marked conditional above is not a weakness; skip the criteria that depend on it.

## Procedure

Work through every criterion below. The checklist is your internal rubric — only findings appear in the output.

### Checklist

- **Chapter goal**: The POV character wants something specific at the chapter's opening.
- **Obstacle / complication**: Something stands in the way. The character cannot simply get what they want.
- **Emotional shift driven by internal conflict**: The character's internal state at chapter's end differs from the start, and that shift is driven by genuine psychological tension — competing desires, conflicting beliefs, or instincts at war — not just external events happening to the character. The chapter plot's Internal Conflict section should identify what the character is wrestling with, and the planned beats should stage that wrestling — this is a planning assessment; no draft exists yet.
- **Arc position accuracy**: The chapter's psychological movement matches its planned position in the book plot's thematic arc map. If this chapter is supposed to carry a turning point, it delivers one. If it's supposed to deepen existing tension without resolving it, it doesn't resolve prematurely.
- **Forward momentum**: The chapter ends with new information, a decision, or an unresolved question. The reader has a reason to continue.
- **Freshness of the turn**: The chapter's turn is not the obvious one. If the outcome a reader would predict at the chapter's opening is exactly what the plan delivers — the suspect is guilty, the argument ends the friendship, the plan fails the expected way — the chapter is confirming expectations instead of turning them.
- **Thread presence**: At least one major thread (from the book's thread list) advances or is meaningfully touched.
- **Word count target**: Uses the chapter word-count range specified in `Plot/Specs.md` as a planning guide. Individual chapters may go under or over when the material earns it; flag deviations only when they create pacing, proportion, or book-length problems.
- **Genre or story-system consistency**: Any magic, technology, politics, law, profession, institution, relationship rule, mystery logic, social system, or other governing rules in the chapter follow established project rules. No new capability or rule appears without prior setup.
- **Terminology and perspective**: Project-specific terms and event naming reflect the POV character's allegiance, culture, and knowledge.

## Output Format

```
## Chapter Plot Assessment: [Chapter Name]

**Strengths** (ranked)
1. ...

**Weaknesses** (ranked by severity)
1. ...

**Verdict**: Approved / Needs revision
```

Concise, ranked, actionable. If the verdict is Needs revision, weaknesses must include specific enough detail to act on.
