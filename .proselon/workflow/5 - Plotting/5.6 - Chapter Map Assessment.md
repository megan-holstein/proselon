# Chapter Map Assessment

Evaluate chapter allocation across a book or chapter batch before creating individual chapter plot documents. Run this rubric only when the book plot is in place.

## Purpose

A chapter map assessment checks whether the planned chapter sequence distributes story material clearly, escalates properly, and gives future chapter-plotting agents a usable allocation plan. It does not assess scene-level execution.

## Prerequisites

Read these files before assessing:

1. `Plot/Book N/Book N Chapter Map.md` — the target chapter map
2. `Plot/Book N/Book N Plot.md` — the book plot
3. `Plot/Series Plot.md` — for series-level context and carry-forward obligations
4. `Plot/Specs.md` — chapter count, word count, and pacing guidelines
5. Project character profiles for major characters in the mapped section
6. `Plot/Themes and Conflict.md` — for thematic thread tracking

## Procedure

Work through every criterion below. The checklist is your internal rubric; only findings appear in the output.

### Checklist

- **Source fidelity**: The map reflects the book plot and does not quietly add, remove, or contradict major plot commitments.
- **Complete allocation**: Every major book-level event, relationship turn, mystery reveal, thematic beat, and carry-forward thread has a clear chapter home.
- **Chapter function clarity**: Each chapter has a distinct job in the sequence. No chapter exists only as atmosphere, aftermath, or repeated hesitation unless that function creates new pressure.
- **Causal flow**: Chapter events follow from prior pressure, decision, discovery, or consequence. Major turns should not feel arbitrary or merely convenient.
- **Escalation**: Stakes, danger, emotional cost, and narrowing options increase across the mapped section. The middle should not flatten into repeated investigation, debate, or delay.
- **Turning-point placement**: Act turns, midpoint shifts, crises, climaxes, and resolution beats fall in structurally plausible places for the book's target length and genre.
- **Load balance**: Chapters are not overloaded with too many major reveals or underloaded with too little change. Overloaded chapters are acceptable only when clearly intended as major turns.
- **Thread spacing**: Major threads recur often enough that they remain alive, but not so often that they stall the main plot.
- **Relationship progression**: Key relationships change in legible steps, especially relationships that later carry betrayal, reconciliation, or thematic payoff.
- **Psychological turning-point distribution**: Key moments where characters' internal conflicts deepen, shift, or break are distributed across the book rather than bunched at the end. The reader should feel psychological pressure building progressively.
- **Internal conflict visibility**: The chapter map's arc progression notes show a legible pattern of psychological escalation, not just plot escalation. If a long stretch of chapters carries no psychological turning points, the middle may feel psychologically static even if events are happening.
- **Mystery and reveal logic**: Discoveries arrive in an order that preserves curiosity, avoids premature answers, and gives later revelations enough setup.
- **POV and knowledge discipline**: The map does not require a character to know, witness, or infer something outside their plausible access unless a deliberate POV shift is planned.
- **Terminology and perspective**: Project-specific terms, offices, factions, and historical events are named according to the relevant character's culture and knowledge.
- **Next-cascade readiness**: The map gives enough allocation detail to create the next act or chapter batch of individual chapter plot documents.

## Output Format

```markdown
## Chapter Map Assessment: [Book / Mapped Section]

**Strengths** (ranked)
1. ...

**Weaknesses** (ranked by severity)
1. ...

**Required Revisions**
- [Only include if verdict is Needs revision. Each item should be specific enough to edit the map.]

**Next Cascade Recommendation**
- [Which act or chapter batch should receive individual chapter plot documents next, or what must be revised first.]

**Verdict**: Approved / Needs revision
```

Keep the assessment concise, ranked, and actionable. If the verdict is Needs revision, weaknesses must identify specific chapters, missing threads, or allocation problems.
