# Chapter Map Template

Use this template after the book plot exists **and has been attacked** — the Synopsis Cold Read and Plot Stress Test reports (`Plot/Book N/Synopsis Cold Read.md`, `Plot/Book N/Plot Stress Test.md`) must exist with findings resolved — before creating individual chapter plot documents.

A chapter map is an allocation document. It is more detailed than the book plot's chapter outline, but less detailed than a full `Chapter N Plot.md`. Its job is to validate what story material belongs in which chapter before the workflow commits to full chapter-level planning.

Before filling it, read the Open Threads section of `Plot/Book N/Story State.md` (if the ledger exists — for Book 2 onward it holds threads carried from the previous book even before any scene of this book completes): every planted promise needs a planned payoff chapter in the map, rather than depending on memory.

## Interview Goals

Before filling the template, gather enough information to answer:

- Is the full book being mapped, or is a smaller chapter batch in scope?
- What is the full chapter sequence for the book?
- What plot material belongs in each chapter?
- What changes by the end of each chapter?
- What needs to be seeded for later chapters, books, or payoffs?
- Which characters or factions appear or matter?
- Which chapters seem overloaded, underloaded, redundant, or out of order?
- Which chapters carry major psychological turning points from the book plot's thematic arc map?
- Is the internal conflict escalation distributed well, or bunched at the end?
- What allocation questions remain open?

Default to mapping the whole book. Use a smaller map only when the book is unusually long, structurally unstable, or the author wants to validate a shorter opening sequence before committing to the full book map.

Do not create individual chapter plot files, scene plots, or manuscript prose until the chapter map is saved.

## Candidate Generation

Produce this artifact via candidates, not a single draft (see AGENTS.md › Candidate Generation for Planning Artifacts) — reveal ordering and pacing are exactly where independent candidates diverge most usefully, and every chapter plot inherits the map's allocation. Force the candidates apart in their briefs: different reveal orderings, different act-boundary placements, different thread-interleaving strategies, one non-obvious variant (a discovery arriving early, a payoff deferred past its expected chapter). The book plot's committed beats are constraints every candidate honors; the map's freedom is *when* and *from which direction*, and that is where the candidates should differ.

## Completion Criteria

A usable chapter map:

- Covers the book or chapter batch in scope.
- Shows what plot material belongs in each chapter.
- Identifies chapter-level changes without fully plotting scenes.
- Tracks major seeds and payoffs.
- Flags overloaded, underloaded, redundant, or misordered chapters.
- Records unresolved allocation questions instead of hiding them.
- Shows where psychological turning points from the thematic arc map fall in the chapter sequence.

**Output saves to:** `Plot/Book N/Book N Chapter Map.md`
