# Title Research

Select a final title for the book based on genre conventions, thematic fit, and competitive positioning. This procedure requires a completed or near-complete manuscript and an existing `Plot/Specs.md`.

## Prerequisites

- `Plot/Specs.md` exists (genre conventions, comp titles, and KDP categories are established).
- `Plot/Themes and Conflict.md` exists (the canonical source for the book's themes and central concepts).
- The manuscript is drafted far enough that the book's core themes, central images, and key concepts are settled.

## Phase 1 — Genre Convention Analysis

Research title conventions for the book's KDP categories and comp titles.

Document:

- **Title length norms** for the genre (one word, two words, phrase)
- **Title type norms** (conceptual, character name, setting, thematic, object, action)
- **Comp title patterns** (what the comp titles have in common structurally)
- **What signals genre** (which kinds of titles tell a browser "this is [genre]" at a glance)
- **What to avoid** (title patterns that signal a different genre or audience)

## Phase 2 — Candidate Generation

Generate a shortlist of 5–10 candidate titles drawn from the book's themes, key concepts, central images, technology, and character dynamics.

For each candidate, note:

- **Source** — what element of the book it comes from
- **Genre signal** — what it tells a browser about the reading experience
- **Tone** — whether it matches the book's tone and atmosphere
- **Thumbnail readability** — whether it works visually on a KDP cover at small size (short titles with strong consonants read better)

## Phase 3 — Competitor Analysis

For each candidate on the shortlist, search the Kindle Store for existing books with the same or very similar titles.

For each competitor found, document:

- **Title and author**
- **Genre/category** — whether it occupies the same categories this book targets
- **Visibility** — rough sense of the competitor's prominence (bestseller rank, review count, publisher size)
- **Risk assessment** — whether this competitor would cause search confusion, audience overlap, or discoverability problems

Narrow the shortlist to 2–4 candidates with the cleanest competitive fields.

## Phase 4 — Title Selection

Walk the author through the narrowed shortlist with the competitor analysis for each option. The author selects the final title.

After selection:

1. Update the title in `Plot/Specs.md`.
2. Update any other project files that reference the title (book plot, KDP listing, etc.) if the author requests it.

## Completion Criteria

- [ ] Genre title conventions documented
- [ ] At least 5 candidates generated with rationale
- [ ] Competitor analysis completed for shortlisted candidates
- [ ] Author has selected a final title
- [ ] `Plot/Specs.md` updated with the selected title
