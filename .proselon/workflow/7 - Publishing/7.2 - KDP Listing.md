# KDP Listing

Compile the metadata that goes into Amazon KDP's listing form: title and subtitle, the description blurb readers see on the product page, the 3 browse categories, the 7 keyword phrases, and the author bio. This procedure runs after Title Research and before Cover Design, since the cover concept benefits from a finalized blurb and positioning.

## Prerequisites

- `Plot/Specs.md` exists (categories, comp titles, genre conventions, target audience).
- Title is selected and recorded in `Plot/Specs.md` (Title Research is complete).
- `Plot/Book N/Book N Plot.md` exists (for blurb content — premise, stakes, character).
- `Plot/Themes and Conflict.md` exists (for thematic register in the blurb and keyword rationale).
- `Style/Style Guide.md` and `Style/Voice Reference.md` exist (for blurb voice).
- The manuscript is drafted far enough that the book's tone, central images, and key beats are settled (ideally fully drafted).

## Phase 1 — Load Prerequisites

Read all prerequisite files. Note: genre, comp titles, target audience, central themes, tone, protagonist, central conflict, and the book's distinguishing format or hook (if any).

## Phase 2 — Confirm Title and Subtitle

Confirm the selected title from `Plot/Specs.md`. Ask the author whether a subtitle is desired. Subtitles are optional and genre-dependent — common in nonfiction, less common in literary fiction, occasional in genre fiction when used as a series marker or hook.

## Phase 3 — Draft the Description Blurb

The blurb is the highest-leverage piece of marketing copy in the listing. It must:

- Hook in the first 1–2 lines (the part visible before "Read more")
- Establish premise, protagonist, and central conflict in 2–3 paragraphs
- Match the book's tone and voice
- Signal genre to category browsers
- Reference comp titles or audience explicitly if it sharpens positioning
- End with stakes or a closing line that creates desire to read

Draft length should match genre convention — literary fiction blurbs tend to be longer and more atmospheric, genre fiction blurbs shorter and more punchy.

Produce **two versions** of the blurb:

1. **Markdown version** — for the saved listing file, using markdown emphasis (`*italics*`, `**bold**`).
2. **HTML version** — for pasting into KDP's listing form. KDP supports a limited HTML subset: `<b>`, `<i>`, `<br>`, `<p>`, `<ul>`, `<li>`, `<h4>-<h6>`. Use `<br><br>` for paragraph breaks (the form does not render `\n\n`).

## Phase 4 — Confirm or Refine Categories

The 3 KDP categories from `Plot/Specs.md` were chosen during Book Specs based on genre conventions. Revisit them now that the book is written:

- Have the categories proved out — does the finished book actually fit each one?
- Is there a smaller, less-competitive category in which this book could chart higher?
- Are any of the chosen categories overcrowded with established authors where this book is unlikely to gain visibility?

For each category, document a rationale tied to the specific book — not generic genre fit, but why *this book* belongs in *this category*.

If any category needs to change, update `Plot/Specs.md` to reflect the final selection.

## Phase 5 — Generate Keywords

KDP allows 7 keyword phrases. Each phrase can be 2–5 words (sometimes more) and should target distinct discovery paths.

Effective keyword strategy combines:

- **Format / structure terms** when the book has a distinctive form (e.g., "epistolary fiction," "dual timeline," "text message novel")
- **Genre + trope** combinations (e.g., "enemies to lovers small town romance")
- **Niche / underserved categories** (e.g., specific mental health representation, niche subgenres, identity-specific terms)
- **BookTok / discovery language** — current high-traffic phrases readers actually search
- **Comp-adjacent searches** — "books like [author]" or "for fans of [author]"
- **Audience-specific terms** (e.g., "queer fiction," "new adult," "coming of age")

For each of the 7 phrases, document a rationale: why this phrase, what discovery path it serves, what search behavior it targets.

Avoid:

- Single common words (Amazon already indexes the title and description)
- Category names that are already a selected category (redundant)
- Subjective claims ("best book ever")
- Other authors' names as standalone keywords (Amazon prohibits this)

## Phase 6 — Author Bio

Ask the author for:

- **Author name** (pen name or real name as they want it published)
- **Bio content** — a 1–3 sentence bio. The bio should match the book's voice when possible; a literary author writes their bio differently than a thriller author.
- **Optional**: a tagline, focus area, or "writes about" statement

Draft the bio based on author input. Keep it short. Most successful debut author bios are 2 sentences or less.

## Phase 7 — Compile and Save

Save the result as `Publishing/Book N KDP Listing.md` using the output template below.

## Phase 8 — Confirm

Show the author the compiled listing and revise based on their feedback.

- [ ] Title and subtitle confirmed
- [ ] Description blurb drafted in both markdown and KDP HTML versions
