# KDP Upload and Pricing

Walk-through for publishing the finished ebook on Amazon KDP: creating the title, entering the saved listing metadata, setting rights, pricing, and enrollment decisions, and verifying the live listing. The author performs these steps in the KDP dashboard — the agent walks them through each screen, supplies the prepared metadata, and advises on the decisions.

**Provenance rule** (same as Title Research and KDP Listing): KDP's terms — price bands, royalty rates, delivery fees, KDP Select rules, review times — change. Verify current terms via web search before advising on them this session; anything stated from model knowledge is tagged `(unverified)` and the author is told to confirm it on the KDP page in front of them. The figures below were correct as of this document's writing.

## Before You Start

- The formatted ebook file exists (`7.4 - Ebook Formatting.md` complete — EPUB from the formatting tool, or the `.docx` on the minimal path)
- The final cover image is chosen and upscaled to KDP dimensions (`7.3 - Ebook Cover.md` complete)
- `Publishing/Book N KDP Listing.md` exists — every field below comes from it
- The author has a KDP account at kdp.amazon.com with tax and bank details completed (one-time setup; walk them through it if not)

## Procedure

### 1. Create the Title and Enter Metadata

On the KDP Bookshelf, choose **Create → Kindle eBook**. From the saved KDP Listing file, enter:

- **Title and subtitle** — exactly as recorded
- **Author name** — the pen name from the listing
- **Description** — paste the **HTML version** of the blurb (the markdown version will not render)
- **Publishing rights** — "I own the copyright" (the author wrote it)

### 2. Categories and Keywords

- **Categories** — enter the 3 categories from the listing file. Amazon's picker may not show the exact browse paths researched; choose the closest match and note any substitution in the listing file.
- **Keywords** — enter the 7 phrases from the listing file, one per field (each already fits the 50-character limit).

### 3. DRM

Recommend **DRM off**. DRM does not stop piracy in practice, but it does stop legitimate buyers from moving the book between their own devices — and the choice is permanent for this edition `(unverified — confirm on the KDP page)`. The author decides; off is the standard indie choice.

### 4. Upload Files and Preview

Upload the manuscript file and the cover image. Run KDP's **online previewer** and have the author check chapter breaks, front and back matter, and the cover render before continuing.

### 5. Territories

Keep the default: **worldwide rights**. Narrow only if the author has sold rights for specific territories to someone else — for a self-published debut, they haven't.

### 6. Pricing and Royalty

KDP has two royalty tiers `(verify current bands)`:

- **70%** — list price $2.99–$9.99, minus a per-megabyte delivery fee (negligible for a text-only novel, meaningful for image-heavy books)
- **35%** — any price outside that band; no delivery fee

Price inside the 70% band unless there's a deliberate reason not to. Genre-typical debut pricing: **$2.99–$4.99** for genre fiction (romance, fantasy, thriller), somewhat higher for literary fiction. A $0.99 launch price drops to the 35% tier — it buys visibility at roughly a seventh of the per-copy royalty, so treat it as a time-boxed promotion, not a resting price. Check what the book's comp titles currently charge and position with them.

Show the math for the chosen price: `royalty = (list price − delivery fee) × 70%`.

### 7. KDP Select / Kindle Unlimited

Decide enrollment before publishing. KDP Select trades **90-day ebook exclusivity to Amazon** (no Kobo, Apple Books, library apps, or even the author's own site) for Kindle Unlimited page-read income and promo tools (Countdown Deals, free days). It auto-renews unless switched off `(verify current terms)`.

- **Select suits a debut** in KU-heavy genres — romance, fantasy, LitRPG, thrillers — where borrowers outnumber buyers and the author has no existing audience on other stores.
- **Go wide** when building a long-term multi-store presence, when the genre skews literary, or when the author objects to exclusivity on principle.

For most Proselon debuts, a first 90-day Select term is a cheap experiment: the data says whether KU pays before the author commits longer. The author decides.

### 8. Pre-order (Optional)

A pre-order makes the listing live before the release date, giving the author a real Amazon link for marketing; the final file must be uploaded ~3 days before release `(verify current lead time)`. Useful when the author has an audience to point at it — a newsletter, a social following, a prior book's readers. A first book with no audience gains little; publishing immediately is simpler.

### 9. Publish and Wait for Review

The author clicks **Publish Your Kindle eBook**. KDP review typically takes up to **72 hours** (usually much less); the book goes live shortly after approval and Amazon emails confirmation.

### 10. Post-Publish Checks

Once live, have the author open the product page and verify together:

- Cover, title, and blurb render correctly (HTML paragraph breaks intact)
- Categories show correctly under "Product details" / the category ladder
- **Look Inside** works and opens on clean front matter
- Price and KU badge (if enrolled) are as chosen

Record the live product URL in `Publishing/Book N KDP Listing.md`. If anything is wrong, fix it in the KDP dashboard — edits go through the same review cycle.
