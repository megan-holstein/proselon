# Ebook Cover

Research cover conventions in the book's genre and categories on Kindle, then produce three distinct image-generation prompts the author can use with ChatGPT to generate cover concepts. The author pastes each prompt into a separate ChatGPT thread, reviews the results, and selects the strongest direction.

## Before You Start

Load the following files and read them fully before proceeding:

- `Plot/Specs.md` — genre, categories, comp titles, target audience, reader promise
- `Plot/Series Plot.md` — series-level premise and themes
- The approved book plot (`Plot/Book N/Book N Plot.md`) — this book's hook, arcs, tone, and emotional core
- `Publishing/Book N KDP Listing.md` — description blurb, keywords, and positioning

Confirm that the KDP listing (`Publishing/Book N KDP Listing.md`) exists before beginning.

## Procedure

### 1. Load Prerequisites

Read all prerequisite files listed above. Note the genre, comp titles, tone, central themes, and target reader.

### 2. Comp Cover Analysis

Research current bestselling and top-ranked covers in the book's KDP categories. Focus on:

- **Visual patterns** — What imagery dominates? (typography-only, illustrated, photographic, abstract, object-focused, character-focused, etc.)
- **Color palettes** — What tones and color schemes signal this genre to readers? (dark/moody, bright/poppy, muted/literary, neon/bold, etc.)
- **Typography style** — Serif vs. sans-serif, hand-lettered vs. clean, size and placement of title vs. author name.
- **Composition** — Where does the eye go? How much whitespace? Centered vs. asymmetric layouts.
- **Mood and tone** — What feeling do the top covers communicate at thumbnail size?

Note which patterns are genre conventions (readers expect them) vs. which are opportunities to stand out while still signaling genre.

### 3. Genre Positioning Analysis

Based on the comp analysis and the book's specific positioning, determine:

- What must the cover communicate at thumbnail size to attract the target reader?
- What visual elements signal this book's genre, tone, and themes?
- What differentiates this book from its comps, and how can the cover reflect that?
- What should the cover avoid? (e.g., imagery that signals the wrong genre, overly literal depictions, cliches that undermine the book's tone)

### 4. Draft Three Cover Prompts

Write three distinct image-generation prompts, each exploring a different creative direction. Each prompt should:

- Describe a complete cover concept — imagery, composition, color palette, mood, and typography direction.
- Be specific enough to produce a usable result in ChatGPT's image generator (include aspect ratio, style references, and concrete visual details).
- Stay within genre conventions enough to signal the correct category to readers, while offering a distinct creative angle.
- Specify that the image should work as a book cover at both full size and thumbnail.
- Include the book title and author name as text to render on the cover.

The three prompts should explore meaningfully different directions — not minor variations of the same idea. For example, one might be typography-driven, another object/symbol-focused, and another atmospheric/textural.

**Resolution note:** Each prompt must request output at the minimum resolution required by KDP for ebook covers (2,560 x 1,600 pixels at 300 DPI for the recommended size). Generating images at lower resolution will produce a low-quality cover.

### 5. Save

Save the analysis and prompts to `Publishing/Book N Ebook Cover.md` using the output template below.

### 6. Handoff to the Author

Give the three prompts to the author. They will paste each into a separate ChatGPT thread, generate concepts, and select the strongest direction. Revise prompts based on their feedback if requested.
