# Whole-Book Read

Tier: craft — reader and structural judgment at book scale; run with the strongest available model.

Every other read in the pipeline tops out below the book: scene, chapter, act. Nothing else ever verifies that the book works *as a book* — that the middle doesn't sag, that the escalation curve holds, that promises planted in Chapter 3 pay off in a way that lands *as read* (the ledger tracks that payoffs are present, not that they satisfy), that the climax is proportionate to its build, that the ending lands, that thirty individually-approved chapters don't share one shape. This pass is that verification, and it is the gate into Step 7: **publishing does not begin until the Whole-Book Read clears.**

## When It Runs

After the book's final chapter completes the full pipeline (through Proof, Ledger, and its chapter-scope Cold Read). Two instruments, run in parallel.

## Instrument 1 — The Reader Panel

Three **Readers** in separate fresh contexts. Each loads **the entire manuscript in reading order — and nothing else**: no plan documents, no ledger, no style files. Each carries a one-line persona from the orchestrating agent, per the Cold Read's panel rules (reading disposition only — a persona brief must contain no story facts): the **target reader**, the **impatient reader**, the **genre-savvy reader**.

Each Reader reads the whole book at reading pace and reports:

- **Momentum** — where did you stall? Where did you consider putting it down? Where could you not stop? Map the sag honestly, especially the middle third.
- **Cumulative predictability** — every major turn you called, and how many chapters early. The reveal you predicted in Chapter 8 that landed in Chapter 20 is a central finding.
- **Promises and payoffs** — which planted questions kept you reading, which you stopped caring about, and whether each payoff *satisfied* — not whether it occurred, whether it landed. Anything you were still waiting for when the book ended.
- **Climax proportion** — did the climax spend what the book built? Too small for its setup, or arriving before the book earned it?
- **The ending** — did it land? What did you feel on the last page? Would you buy the next book — and what question would make you?
- **Sameness** — chapters or scenes that blur together; the moves this book repeats; the shape you started recognizing.
- **Feeling** — the three moments in the whole book that landed hardest, named specifically. Feeling is load-bearing at book scale exactly as at scene scale: a book without landed moments fails this read no matter how clean it is.
- **Confusion**, as in every Cold Read — anything you had to reread or never resolved.

Attest every check, one line each, even when clean. Verdict per Reader: **Approved** or **Needs revision**, with the single finding that most damages the book. Testimony merges attributed, never averaged; the panel verdict is the majority.

## Instrument 2 — Book-Scope Developmental Assessment

One fresh craft context — the plan-holding counterpart. **Loads:** `Plot/Book N/Book N Plot.md`, `Plot/Themes and Conflict.md`, `Plot/Series Plot.md`, the final `Plot/Book N/Story State.md` (Open Threads and Established Facts especially), and the manuscript. Findings-only. Verify:

- **Arc delivery** — the book that got written delivers the book plot's arc: inciting disruption, escalation, crisis, climax, resolution, each present *in the prose* and carrying the planned weight.
- **Thread settlement** — every Open Threads entry is paid on the page, or deliberately carried forward with its hook still alive. Nothing silently dropped; nothing paid so quietly a reader would miss it.
- **Thematic delivery** — each theme's planned arc is legible in the manuscript through character and event, not packaging; each major character's internal-conflict arc completes (or deliberately holds) as designed.
- **Series handoff** — the ending leaves the series plot's next-book obligations intact and the carry-forward promises planted.

## Routing and Re-Check

The orchestrating agent merges panel testimony with the developmental findings and routes: line-scale findings to 6.4-style handling for the scenes named; scene- and chapter-scale structural findings to 6.2-style revision (re-entering the normal pipeline, including re-voicing where prose is regenerated); sag, sameness, and payoff findings that implicate *chapter plots* surface to the author with a recommendation — at book scale, most load-bearing findings are creative decisions, and the author decides scope.

After revisions: **one bounded re-check.** If the changes were local (named scenes, a chapter), one fresh Reader re-reads the affected chapters in context (the surrounding two chapters on each side) plus the developmental assessor re-verifying the touched threads. If the changes were structural (chapters added, moved, or rewritten), the full panel re-reads the book. One re-check, then residual findings become Concerns for the author — never an open loop on a whole manuscript.

## The Gate File

Save the final merged report — panel testimony summaries, developmental findings, verdicts, what was revised, residual Concerns — to `Plot/Book N/Whole-Book Read.md`. That file existing with a clearing verdict is the Step 7 gate: state detection treats a book without it as still in Step 6.
