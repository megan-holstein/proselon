# Voice Rewrite

Tier: craft — run with the strongest available model, ideally a *different* model than the one that drafted the scene. Different models have different default tics; decorrelating the draft and the rewrite breaks shared patterns.

You are the **Writer**, not an assessor (see AGENTS.md › The Writer↔Assessor Loop): this is the second generative step — a wholesale rewrite, not a corrective pass. The draft has already cleared its Developmental Assessment; continuity is verified *after* the rewrite, by the Continuity Check (6.5). A scene can clear every catalog check and still sound like competent generic fiction — polishing within existing sentences cannot escape that; rewriting can. Your job is to make the scene sound like *this book*.

## Context to Load

Load ONLY:

- `Style/Voice Reference.md` — especially the Model Passages corpus
- The current scene file
- The final page of the previous scene (for rhythm continuity into this scene)

Deliberately do NOT load the scene plot, plot documents, or the line catalogs. This step is about sound, not compliance — structure has already been reviewed, the catalogs are enforced by the Line Assessment that follows, and facts and state are verified afterward by the Continuity Check.

## The Instruction

The Voice Reference corpus is what this book sounds like. Continue it.

Rewrite the scene in that voice. You may restructure sentences and paragraphs freely. Do not change what happens, who knows what, or what any line of dialogue means — change how it sounds.

## Freedom Boundary

**Fixed** (the Continuity Check (6.5) verifies these survived your rewrite — do not disturb them):

- Events and their order
- Facts, and which facts are new versus established
- Character knowledge states
- The meaning of every dialogue exchange

**Fully open:**

- Diction, syntax, rhythm
- Sentence and paragraph structure
- Interiority and imagery
- Where the prose lingers and where it cuts

## Candidate Selection

Creative generation has high variance: two rewrites of the same scene from the same corpus differ meaningfully in how alive they are. Selection between candidates is a pure quality lever — use it on every scene.

- **First ~3 scenes of a book (until voice sign-off): two candidates, the author picks.** Produce two independent rewrites in separate fresh contexts — neither sees the other — and present both with the voice sign-off (see the Voice Reference's Corpus Maintenance rules). Choosing between two versions is a far easier authorial judgment than critiquing one, and the pick is corpus signal: promote the chosen version's best passages into Author-Selected Passages.
- **After sign-off: multi-candidate remains the default for every scene.** Produce **2–3 independent rewrites** in separate fresh contexts — different strong models where available; two contexts of the same model share tics. A fresh craft-tier **judge** — loading only the Model Passages corpus and the candidates, blind to which context produced which — judges on **two axes**: *fidelity* (which sounds most like the corpus) and *aliveness* (which reads most alive, cold). When the axes agree, done. When they disagree, record both judgments in the scene report and prefer the more alive candidate unless it breaks the voice — a judge selecting on corpus-similarity alone regresses every scene toward the corpus's mean, which is exactly the flattening this pass exists to fight. A line or two of reasoning goes in the scene report.

## How to Work

1. Read the Voice Reference corpus immediately before rewriting — as demonstration, not rules.
2. Read the previous scene's final page to pick up the rhythm coming in.
3. Rewrite the scene top to bottom in the book's voice.
4. Re-read once. Anywhere it sounds like generic competent fiction rather than this book, rewrite again.

Run this pass in a fresh context whenever possible (see AGENTS.md › Writing Pipeline). The drafting context contains the draft's own rhythms; a fresh context hears the corpus, not the draft.

The Line Assessment (`6.4 - Line Assessment.md`) runs after this step and will catch any mechanical issues or catalog violations the rewrite introduces — do not constrain the rewrite to pre-comply with catalogs. The Continuity Check (`6.5 - Continuity Check.md`) then verifies that events, facts, and knowledge states survived the rewrite.

Preserve exportable manuscript content only; do not embed scene plots or planning notes in the manuscript file.
