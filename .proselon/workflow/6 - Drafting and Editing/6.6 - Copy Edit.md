# Copy Edit

Tier: mechanical — pattern-matching against explicit criteria; a fast, inexpensive model is adequate.

This is a mechanics and consistency pass. The prose is polished — you are not changing voice, rhythm, or word choice. You are checking grammar, punctuation, spelling, usage, and the consistency of names, terms, and formatting conventions across the scene.

## Before You Start

Have these files loaded for reference:

- The project **style guide** (`Style/Style Guide.md`)
- Project **character profiles** for every character in the scene (for name spellings)
- Project **terminology and worldbuilding references** (for in-world term spellings, capitalization, and hyphenation conventions)
- Any project **style sheet** that records previously-decided hyphenation, capitalization, or term conventions

## What to Check

### Grammar

- Subject-verb agreement
- Pronoun antecedents — every pronoun should have an unambiguous referent
- Dangling and misplaced modifiers
- Parallel structure in lists and series
- Verb forms (lay/lie, sit/set, raise/rise)

### Punctuation

- Comma splices and run-on sentences
- Commas in compound sentences, after introductory phrases, around non-restrictive clauses
- Semicolons used correctly (joining independent clauses, or separating list items containing commas) — but never *introduce* a semicolon the prose didn't have: the AI-tendencies catalog treats semicolons in fiction as a tell, and the Line Assessment may have removed them deliberately
- Em dashes use ` — ` (space, em dash, space) for parenthetical asides and interrupted speech — a default that yields to the style guide if it specifies closed em dashes
- Ellipses are three dots with no spaces between: `...`
- Hyphens for compound modifiers before a noun ("a long-overdue letter") but not after ("the letter was long overdue")
- Apostrophes for possessives and contractions only — never for plurals
- No double spaces after periods

### Dialogue Mechanics

- Comma before the closing quote when followed by a tag: `"Yes," he says.`
- Period when followed by action: `He nods. "Yes."`
- New paragraph for each new speaker
- Em dash for interrupted speech: `"I thought —"` `"So did I."`
- Ellipsis for trailing off: `"I don't..."`
- Dialogue attribution is unambiguous — it should always be clear who is speaking, even without a tag

### Tense Consistency

The project style guide determines the required narrative tense. Read every verb.

- All narrative verbs must match the project's required tense
- Other tenses are correct only when the timeline requires them (pluperfect for prior events, future for anticipated ones)
- Watch for slips in dialogue tags and action beats after dialogue

### Spelling and Usage

- Homophones: their/there/they're, its/it's, who's/whose, your/you're, then/than, affect/effect, lay/lie, peek/peak/pique
- Possessives: especially names ending in `s` (Charles's or Charles', per style guide)
- Commonly confused words per the style guide

### Consistency (Style Sheet Enforcement)

- **Character name spellings** — every reference uses the canonical form
- **Made-up term spellings** — every reference uses the canonical form
- **Hyphenation choices** — decided once per term, applied consistently throughout the scene and across scenes (e.g., "starlight" vs. "star-light" vs. "star light")
- **Capitalization** of in-world terms, titles, factions, places, and ranks — per style guide
- **Numbers** — when to spell out vs. use numerals, per style guide

### Formatting Conventions

Defaults — each yields to the project style guide where it specifies otherwise:

- Italics for direct internal thought: `*thought text*`
- Em dashes use ` — ` (spaces around)
- Single line breaks between paragraphs

## Style Sheet Maintenance

When you encounter a term, name spelling, hyphenation choice, or capitalization decision that is not yet documented in the project style guide and you have made a call about how to render it, report it in the final response so it can be added to the style guide. Consistency holds across the manuscript only if every decision lives in the reference material.

## How to Make Changes

Fix mechanical errors silently. Do not add comments for routine corrections — just make them. If you find a usage or consistency issue where the correct answer isn't clear from the style guide, report the ambiguity in the final response.

Preserve exportable manuscript content only; do not embed scene plots or planning notes in the manuscript file.
