# Series Refresh

Tier: craft — series-level story judgment; run with the strongest available model.

The series plot is written before Book 1 exists, and the template deliberately keeps later books loose — detail forced that early goes stale. Which means the series plot **must** be re-grounded when each book ships: Book N+1 otherwise gets planned against a document written before anyone knew what Book N actually became. That drift is the standard cause of book-2 slump, and it compounds silently — nothing else in the workflow re-reads the series plot between books.

## When It Runs

After Book N completes its Whole-Book Read (6.8) and **before** any Book N+1 plotting (5.1). "Ships" throughout this doc means *completes 6.8* — the manuscript is frozen from then on; publication (Step 7) may still be in flight when the refresh runs. This is a gate: state detection treats a book plot for Book N+1 as premature until `Plot/Book N/Series Refresh.md` exists (see Output).

## Procedure

1. **Load:** `Plot/Series Plot.md`, `Plot/Specs.md`, `Plot/Themes and Conflict.md`, the shipped Book N's final chapter map and `Plot/Book N/Story State.md` (Open Threads and end-state especially), `Plot/Book N/Whole-Book Read.md` (the panel's testimony about what actually landed), and — where feasible — the manuscript's final chapters.

2. **Reconcile the plan with the shipped book.** Walk the series plot section by section and mark every claim Book N's reality has confirmed, changed, or broken: threads that resolved differently, characters whose arcs moved further or less far than planned, promises the book actually made (a shipped book plants promises the plan never listed — harvest them from the Whole-Book Read testimony and the ledger), tonal ground the series has actually claimed. Update the series plot; propagate anything that touches `Plot/Themes and Conflict.md` per the Revisions rules.

3. **Elevate Book N+1.** Its section graduates from Plot Shape + Required Developments to a full Plot Summary — written against the *reconciled* series shape, not the original guess. Run this as candidate generation (see AGENTS.md › Candidate Generation for Planning Artifacts) when the shape is genuinely open.

4. **Re-run the Series Plot Assessment (2.2) in full**, as a panel, on the refreshed document — including its two between-books criteria (Read-through hook; Middle-book function), which have real material to bite on only now.

5. **Run the Synopsis Cold Read** on the refreshed series shape (series-level rendering — see the pass doc: one paragraph per shipped book as written, a fuller treatment of Book N+1 as planned) — its prediction test now measures whether a reader who finished Book N would call the rest of the series. Record its testimony and prediction results in the refresh report rather than a separate file.

## Output

- The updated `Plot/Series Plot.md`, with Book N's section marked shipped (a `**Status**: shipped` line at the top of its block — see the 2.1 template's per-book Status line).
- Any propagated updates (Themes and Conflict, character profiles).
- **The refresh report, saved to `Plot/Book N/Series Refresh.md`**: what Book N changed about the series, what Book N+1's elevated function is, the 2.2 panel's verdict, the series-level Synopsis Cold Read testimony and prediction results, and the open creative decisions the refresh surfaced. This file is the refresh's gate artifact — its existence is how state detection confirms the refresh ran before Book N+1 plotting. Present its substance to the author as well; the file is the durable record, not a substitute for the conversation.

Only after this clears does Book N+1 enter Step 5.
