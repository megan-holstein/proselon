# Series Plot Template

Use this template to create or revise the series-level or whole-project plot.

For a standalone novel, treat the series plot as the whole-project story plot and rename sections as needed.

## Interview Goals

Before filling the template, gather enough information to answer:

- What is the project about?
- Where does the story begin emotionally, socially, and externally?
- Where should it end?
- What are the major installments or movements?
- What promises is the story making to the reader?
- What major character, relationship, thematic, mystery, or worldbuilding threads must carry across the whole project?
- What is confirmed, and what is still tentative?

Do not require every future book, episode, or installment to be solved in detail. Capture loose shapes and open questions.

## Completion Criteria

A usable series plot document:

- Identifies the whole-project premise.
- States the beginning and ending conditions.
- Gives every planned installment a clear function.
- Defines the active book in enough detail to support book-level plot and assessment.
- Defines later books by function, required coverage, continuity commitments, and open questions; they do not need full sequential plot yet.
- Tracks major cross-project threads.
- Separates confirmed decisions from open questions.
- Avoids detailed chapter or scene planning.
